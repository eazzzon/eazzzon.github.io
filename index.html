RESEARCH INTERESTS

Hi, I am Eason. I am a postdoc associate at CLEVER Planets at Rice University, USA

My research interests cover three aspects in igneous petrology:
1) Interior structure and geochemical composition of rocky (exo)planets
2) Mantle melting processes
3) Crystallization kinetics in magmatic systems
To carry out my research, I use experimental petrology, thermodynamics, numerical modelling combined with geochemical and textural analysis.

I did my PhD with Olivier Namur, co-supervised with Bernard Charlier. My PhD projects focus on using high temperature high pressure facilities to understand the differentiation and thermal structure of large igneous provinces (LIPs) and 3D textural analysis of minerals to reveal dynamic magmatic processes.


Besides experimental study, I am very enthusiastic about coding and data analysis, where I develop tools for the petrology/volcanology community. Python is my main coding language, I also have a touch on Matlab, LaTeX, shell scripting and HTML.
