ABOUT ME

Hi, I am Eason.

My PhD project is to improve our understanding of Large Igneous Provinces (LIPs) through high temperature and high pressure experiments. Large Igneous Province (LIP) are the products of the largest volcanism in the geological history of the Earth.

The formation of LIPs is still debated due to the diversity of mantle sources and poorly constrained thermal structure. Thus one part of my PhD is to develop new tools to better constrain mantle melting conditions, and especially magma temperature and mantle sources.

Since most LIPs host mafic-ultramafic layered intrusions, these intrusions are always associated with massive Fe-Ti-V oxides deposits, which are the most important sources for the Fe-Ti ores in the world. Another part of my PhD is trying to understand processes responsible for the formation of Fe-Ti-V ores, especially test the role of silicate liquid immiscibility!
